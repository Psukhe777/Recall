Your privacy matters to us.

Recall is a HIPAA-compliant platform built for healthcare professionals. We never sell patient data, never use it for advertising, and go beyond baseline legal requirements to keep every record secure.

1 Overview

Recall Pty Ltd ("Recall", "we", "us", or "our") operates the Recall healthcare automation platform accessible at recallapp.io and related subdomains (the "Service"). This Privacy Policy explains what personal information we collect, why we collect it, how we use it, and your rights as a user.

By accessing or using Recall, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy. If you are a healthcare practice using Recall on behalf of patients, you agree to obtain all necessary consents from those patients before uploading their information to the Service.

This policy applies to:

  • Healthcare providers, practice managers, and staff who create Recall accounts ("Practice Users").
  • Patients whose contact details are uploaded into the platform by a practice ("Patients").
  • Visitors to our marketing website and documentation.

We do not collect sensitive health records.

Recall is a recall and scheduling automation tool. We do not collect clinical notes, diagnoses, medications, or full medical histories. Patient data is limited to what is required to send appointment reminders and process bookings.

3 How We Use Your Data

We use your information only for the purposes described below:

  1. Providing the Service: Creating and managing your account, running automated recall campaigns, processing replies, and generating analytics reports.
  2. Billing and Payments: Processing subscription fees, issuing invoices, and managing plan upgrades or cancellations.
  3. Customer Support: Responding to your support requests, diagnosing issues, and improving product reliability.
  4. Security and Fraud Prevention: Detecting unauthorised access, preventing abuse, and enforcing our Terms of Service.
  5. Product Improvement: Analysing aggregated, anonymised usage patterns to improve features. We never use patient contact data for this purpose.
  6. Legal Compliance: Meeting obligations under Australian Privacy Act 1988 (Cth), HIPAA (for US customers), GDPR (for EU customers), and other applicable laws.
  7. Communications: Sending transactional emails (account alerts, billing receipts) and, with your permission, product newsletters and updates. You can opt out of marketing communications at any time.

We do not use personal data to train machine learning models, serve third-party advertising, or build consumer profiles for resale.

4 SMS Communications

SMS is the core delivery mechanism of the Recall platform. The following terms govern all SMS activity:

  • Consent obligation: Practices are solely responsible for obtaining valid consent from patients before uploading their mobile numbers. By uploading a patient, you represent that you have the legal right to contact them.
  • Opt-out handling: Any patient who replies STOP, UNSUBSCRIBE, CANCEL, END, or QUIT is immediately removed from all future campaigns. Recall processes opt-outs in real time.
  • Message frequency: Patients receive recall messages only when a campaign is actively scheduled by the practice. Recall does not send unsolicited messages.
  • Message content: SMS content is set by the practice. Recall provides templates and enforces content guidelines; we do not transmit content that is unlawful, deceptive, or contrary to applicable carrier regulations.
  • Carrier compliance: All messages are delivered through tier-1 carriers in compliance with TCPA (US), ACMA Spam Act (AU), and equivalent regulations.
  • Message logs: Inbound and outbound SMS logs are stored securely for 12 months and are accessible only to authorised users within your practice account.
⚠
Practice responsibility.

If a patient has requested to stop receiving communications, their number must not be re-uploaded to a new campaign. Practices that repeatedly upload opted-out numbers may have their account suspended.

6 HIPAA & Healthcare Data

🏥
Recall is HIPAA-compliant for US healthcare customers.

We sign Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) with all US-based healthcare providers on our Growth and Enterprise plans. Contact privacy@recallapp.io to request a BAA.

As a Business Associate under HIPAA, Recall commits to the following obligations in relation to Protected Health Information (PHI):

  • Use or disclose PHI only as permitted by the BAA and the HIPAA Privacy Rule.
  • Implement appropriate administrative, physical, and technical safeguards consistent with the HIPAA Security Rule.
  • Report any known breach of unsecured PHI to the covered entity within 60 days of discovery.
  • Not use PHI for independent purposes beyond those required to operate the Service.
  • Ensure that any sub-contractors who handle PHI on our behalf sign equivalent agreements.
  • Return or destroy PHI upon termination of the BAA.

Australian customers are governed by the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs) under the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and, where applicable, the My Health Records Act 2012 (Cth). EU customers are governed by the GDPR — see Section 9 for your rights.

7 Data Security

Security is foundational to Recall. We maintain the following safeguards:

  • Encryption in transit: All data transmitted between your browser, our API, and third-party services is encrypted using TLS 1.2 or higher.
  • Encryption at rest: All databases and file storage use AES-256 encryption at rest on AWS infrastructure in the ap-southeast-2 (Sydney) region.
  • Access controls: Role-based access control (RBAC) restricts which staff can view, export, or modify patient data within your practice account.
  • Multi-factor authentication: MFA is available to all users and required for admin-level accounts.
  • Penetration testing: We engage independent security firms to conduct annual penetration tests. Summary reports are available on request for Enterprise customers.
  • Audit logging: All access to patient records and account changes are logged with timestamps and user identifiers, retained for 24 months.
  • Incident response: We maintain a documented incident response plan. Affected customers will be notified within 72 hours of a confirmed breach involving their data.

No system is 100% secure. If you discover a potential security vulnerability, please report it responsibly to security@recallapp.io. We operate a responsible disclosure programme and will respond within 2 business days.

8 Retention & Deletion

We retain your data only for as long as necessary to provide the Service or meet legal obligations:

SMS message logs 12 months from message date

You may request immediate deletion of your account and all associated patient data at any time by contacting privacy@recallapp.io. We will confirm deletion within 30 days.

9 Your Rights

Depending on your location, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right to access: Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten"): Request deletion of your personal data, subject to legal retention requirements.
  • Right to data portability: Request your data in a structured, machine-readable format (e.g. CSV).
  • Right to restriction: Request that we limit how we process your data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to object: Object to processing based on our legitimate interests, including direct marketing.
  • Right to withdraw consent: Where processing is based on your consent, withdraw it at any time without affecting prior processing.
  • Right to lodge a complaint: If you are in the EU/EEA, you may lodge a complaint with your local Data Protection Authority. Australian users may complain to the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC).

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at privacy@recallapp.io. We will respond within 30 days (or 45 days where permitted by applicable law for complex requests).

10 Cookies & Tracking

We use cookies and similar technologies to operate the platform and improve your experience. The categories we use are:

  • Strictly necessary cookies: Required for authentication, session management, and security. Cannot be disabled.
  • Functional cookies: Remember your preferences (e.g. timezone, language, dashboard layout).
  • Analytics cookies: Collect anonymised data about how features are used, via self-hosted analytics. We do not use Google Analytics on authenticated pages.
  • Marketing cookies: Used only on our public marketing website to measure the effectiveness of paid campaigns. These are not placed on the application dashboard.

You can manage cookie preferences in your browser settings. Disabling functional cookies may affect the usability of some features. We do not use cookies to build advertising profiles or sell your data to advertising networks.

11 Children's Privacy

The Recall platform is designed for use by healthcare professionals. Our Service is not directed at individuals under the age of 16, and we do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If a practice manages appointments for minor patients (e.g. paediatric or veterinary services where the account holder is the parent/guardian), the practice is responsible for ensuring they hold appropriate parental consent.

If we become aware that we have inadvertently collected personal data from a child without appropriate consent, we will take steps to delete that data promptly. Please contact us at privacy@recallapp.io if you believe this has occurred.

12 Policy Changes

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, legal requirements, or other factors. When we make material changes, we will:

  • Post the updated policy on this page with a revised "Last Updated" date.
  • Send an email notification to all active account holders at least 14 days before the changes take effect.
  • Display a banner in the Recall dashboard for 30 days after any material update.

Your continued use of the Service after the effective date of any revised policy constitutes your acceptance of those changes. If you do not agree to the updated terms, you may cancel your account before the effective date.
